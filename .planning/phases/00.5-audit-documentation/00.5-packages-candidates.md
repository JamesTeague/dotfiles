## Methodology

Per CONTEXT.md decision protocol, "dead" determination is **not autonomous**.
Removal recommendations are produced by cross-checking **two signals**:

1. **Installed on the active Mac(s)?** — `brew list --formula` and
   `brew list --cask` enumerate what's actually present. Absence on a daily
   driver is a signal the entry may be unused.
2. **Upstream maintenance status?** — `brew info <pkg>` exposes `deprecated`,
   `disabled`, and last-bottled date for formulae/casks. Abandonware on the
   upstream side is a strong removal signal.

**Both signals together** (not installed AND upstream-stale) yield a
"delete" recommendation. **Either signal alone** yields a "review"
recommendation — Teague decides.

Items that are dead-by-template (currently exist in `packages.yaml` but the
`.chezmoitemplates/brew` rendering never references them — e.g. `work.core.*`
and top-level `personal.*`) are surfaced explicitly; the recommendation is
usually "move" (relocate to a block the template DOES consume) rather than
"delete", since the items themselves are real installs.

## Decision Column

Each row's `Decision` column takes one of:

- `delete` — remove from `packages.yaml` (after Teague approval)
- `keep` — leave in place
- `move` — relocate to a different block (typically dead-path → live-path)
- `defer` — escalate to Phase 0 (touches taxonomy work)

Plan 05 fills `Recommendation`; Teague fills `Decision` during review.
