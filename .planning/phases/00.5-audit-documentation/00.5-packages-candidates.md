## Methodology

Per CONTEXT.md decision protocol, "dead" determination is **not autonomous**. Removal recommendations are produced by cross-checking **two signals**:

1. **Installed on the active Mac(s)?** — `brew list --formula` and `brew list --cask` enumerate what's actually present. Absence on a daily driver is a signal the entry may be unused.
2. **Upstream maintenance status?** — `brew info <pkg>` exposes `deprecated`, `disabled`, naming drift, and last-bottled date for formulae/casks. Abandonware or wrong-name on the upstream side is a strong removal signal.

**Both signals together** (not installed AND upstream-stale/broken) yield a "delete" recommendation. **Either signal alone** yields a "review" recommendation — Teague decides.

Items that are **dead-by-template** (currently exist in `packages.yaml` but the `.chezmoitemplates/brew` rendering never references them — e.g. `work.core.*` and top-level `personal.{taps,brews,casks}`) are surfaced explicitly; the recommendation is usually **move** (relocate to a block the template DOES consume) rather than delete, since the items themselves may be real installs.

**Capture context:** Plan 05 Task 1 ran on Mac personal 2026-05-28T02:43:40Z. `brew list --formula` returned 235 entries; `brew list --cask` returned 45 entries; `brew tap` returned 12 entries. Snapshots saved to `/tmp/installed-formulae.txt`, `/tmp/installed-casks.txt`, `/tmp/installed-taps.txt` for this session.

## Decision Column

Each row's `Decision` column takes one of:

- `delete` — remove from `packages.yaml` (after Teague approval)
- `keep` — leave in place
- `move` — relocate to a different block (typically dead-path → live-path)
- `defer` — escalate to Phase 0 (touches taxonomy work)

Plan 05 fills `Recommendation`; Teague fills `Decision` during review.
